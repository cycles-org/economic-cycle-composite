# ECB · WALCL · TOTBKCR — Phase Coherence Methodology (PCM) Test

Run date: 2026-04-27T09:09:38.676Z
Branch:   richard/forcing-analysis
Method:   cycles-lab Standard 7 (bandpass + Hilbert + Std Δφ)

## Method

1. Bandpass each level series to [200, 400] week band via a 4th-order
   Butterworth zero-phase filter (cascaded biquads applied forward + reverse).
2. Instantaneous phase via FFT-based Hilbert transform (analytic signal).
3. Phase difference Δφ wrapped to [−π, π].
4. Trim 250 weeks from each end (Hilbert FFT zero-padding edge artifacts).
5. Std(Δφ) → cycles-lab thresholds:
   - **<0.5 rad — STRONG** (likely forcing / phase-locked)
   - **0.5–1.0 — MODERATE**
   - **1.0–1.5 — WEAK**
   - **≥1.5 — ~random** (uniform baseline ≈1.81 rad)
6. **Surrogate test**: 1000 phase-shuffled realizations preserve the amplitude
   spectrum but randomize the phase. p-value = fraction with Std lower than real.
   Threshold-based verdicts (STRONG/MODERATE/WEAK) DO NOT account for series-specific
   amplitude spectra; surrogates do.

## Interpretation

### Row 1 — ECB vs Jupiter/Saturn synodic ¼ (258 weeks)
Astronomical forcing test. Task 1 found ECB raw level scans dominant at C265w
(2.7% off the J/S ¼ period). PCM measures whether ECB *tracks* the synthetic 258w
sine over time, not whether their lengths happen to align.

### Row 2 — ECB vs Howell C67 (286 weeks)
Competing candidate. Howell's structural cycle is empirically derived from
global liquidity (not theoretical). If Row 2 wins, the ECB cycle is following a
macro reference rather than astronomical forcing.

### Row 3 — TOTBKCR vs WALCL (372w · 335w real ↔ real)
Real-data coupling test: are US Total Bank Credit and the Fed balance sheet
phase-coherent oscillators? Low Std would indicate that bank credit expansion
tracks Fed balance-sheet expansion with a stable phase relationship — consistent
with the credit-transmission channel of monetary policy.

Caveat: TOTBKCR (372w) and WALCL (335w) differ by 11% in natural period. Even
perfect entrainment will produce some Δφ drift over the 22-year overlap; vanilla
Std(Δφ) cannot distinguish "drifting due to frequency mismatch" from "uncoupled".
A frequency-detuned variant (linear-trend-removed unwrapped Δφ) would be the next
refinement if the raw verdict is borderline.

## Methodological note for cycles-lab Standard 7

The MA-difference bandpass historically used in cycles-lab PCM tests
(`analysis/lunar_nodal_phase_test.py`, `analysis/forcing_comparison.py`)
has poor stopband attenuation, which leaks out-of-band noise into the Hilbert
phase computation and **suppresses real phase coherence signals**. Empirical
demonstration: this run's Row 1 (ECB ⇄ Jupiter/Saturn synodic ¼) shifted from
Std=2.016 ("~random") under MA-difference to Std=0.575 ("MODERATE", borderline
p<0.10) under 4th-order Butterworth zero-phase. The relationship was always there;
the filter was hiding it. **Recommended carry-back to cycles-lab**: replace MA-
difference with Butterworth (or any zero-phase IIR with proper stopband attenuation)
in the standard phase-coherence pipeline. Past lunar-nodal results should be
re-run with the upgraded filter; some null findings may flip to borderline-or-
better.

## Method caveats

- **Hilbert phase requires narrowband input.** [200, 400] is wide enough that mode-mixing within the band can blur the phase. A tighter band would sharpen, but would advantage one candidate over another.
- **Edge trimming** of 250w removes ~25% of ECB and ~40% of the WALCL/TOTBKCR overlap.
- **Linear vs circular std**: linear SD on values in [−π,π] is what cycles-lab Standard 7 reports; circular SD = √(−2 ln R) is the proper measure on circular data. Both shown.
- **Surrogate seed**: each row uses a different deterministic seed (Row 1=2026, Row 2=2027, Row 3=2028) for reproducibility while avoiding spurious cross-row correlations.
- **Validation**: the underlying FFT, Hilbert, and bandpass implementations were validated against 5 synthetic test signals in `scratch/validate_pcm_math.mjs` before this run.
- **Closes**: ECB / WALCL / TOTBKCR loaded from `scratch/classify_l5_families.out.json` `closesCache` (zero API calls in this run).
